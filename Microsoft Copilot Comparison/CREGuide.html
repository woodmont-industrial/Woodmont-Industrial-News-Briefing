How Companies Prioritize

Hyperscalers (Amazon, Google, Meta)

Prioritize power above all else due to grid capacity scarcity. Often partner with energy providers or build on-site generation. Favor low-cost renewable energy for sustainability targets.

Cloud Colocation Providers

Focus on network connectivity and market access, clustering around major internet exchange points and dense fiber infrastructure.

Developer Priorities

Affordable/Build-to-Rent

Balance density with cost. Often select sites allowing 20‚Äì40 units per acre using wood construction (garden apartments, village clusters).

Urban Infill/Transit-Oriented

Prioritize public transit access and high allowable density. Use podium or mid-rise configurations (90‚Äì150 units per acre).

Luxury/High-Rise

Seek premium locations near employment centers. Build 8+ story towers (140‚Äì300 units per acre) to support high land costs.

Russo Development Case Studies

A New Jersey-based developer specializing in mission-critical data centers, industrial distribution facilities, and multifamily projects.

Data Center Projects

777 Central Boulevard (Carlstadt, NJ)

  • Site: ~300,000 sq ft on 12 acres
  • Power: Dedicated 25-MW substation
  • Strategy: Ensured abundant dedicated power for backup operations serving large financial, medical, and technology tenants

760 Washington Avenue (Carlstadt, NJ)

  • Site: 172,000 sq ft with 36-ft ceiling height
  • Power: 5-MW utility capacity
  • Location: ~7 miles from George Washington Bridge and Lincoln Tunnel
  • Strategy: Prioritized proximity to NYC and major fiber routes for enterprise customers

50 Madison Road (Totowa, NJ)

  • Site: 150,000 sq ft with 32-ft ceiling
  • Power: 16-MW utility capacity
  • Location: ~20 miles from NYC
  • Strategy: Significant power and height to handle IT equipment, proximity to metropolitan hubs for latency-sensitive users

Purpose-Built Data Center (Mahwah, NJ)

  • Site: Large greenfield site, 395,000 sq ft
  • Power: Dual 138 kV transmission lines in ring-bus configuration
  • Infrastructure: Dual municipal water mains, dark fiber connectivity
  • Tenant: Major financial institution
  • Strategy: Selected site with abundant high-voltage power and redundant utilities, proximity to Manhattan
üéØ Key Takeaway: Russo emphasizes dedicated power capacity (5‚Äì25 MW), redundant utility infrastructure, and proximity to NYC for connectivity and market access. Buildings feature high clear heights and reinforced floor loads for heavy equipment.
